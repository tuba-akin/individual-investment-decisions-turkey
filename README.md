# Individual Investment Decisions in Turkey Under Uncertainty

## Project Overview

This project is a data-driven case study focused on individual investment decisions in Turkey under macroeconomic uncertainty.

The main objective is not to recommend a single investment instrument, but to build a structured decision-making framework for a regularly employed individual living in Turkey with a 3–5 year investment horizon.

The study analyzes key macroeconomic indicators, major investment instruments, sector-level market performance, volatility, and risk profiles to understand how individual investors can make more resilient decisions in uncertain economic conditions.

## Problem Statement

In recent years, investment decisions have become more complex due to:

- High inflation
- Changing interest rate environment
- Exchange rate volatility
- Geopolitical risks
- Energy and commodity price pressures
- Market uncertainty

In this environment, the main question for individual investors is no longer only:

> “Which asset generates the highest return?”

Instead, the more important question becomes:

> “Which investment approach can remain more resilient across different economic conditions?”

## Scope of the Analysis

The analysis covers the 2021–2026 period in order to observe different macroeconomic regimes, including high inflation, interest rate changes, currency depreciation, and market volatility.

The project focuses on the following areas:

- Inflation, policy rate, and deposit rate trends
- Relative performance of USD/TRY, gram gold, and BIST100
- Sector-level performance differences within Borsa Istanbul
- Approximate real return comparison
- Monthly volatility analysis
- Asset behavior during selected shock periods
- Investor profile-based decision framework

## Data Sources

The study uses publicly available data from reliable sources such as:

- Central Bank of the Republic of Türkiye (CBRT / EVDS)
- Borsa Istanbul
- Historical market data platforms
- Brent oil price data
- Gold and exchange rate data

## Methodology

The project follows a structured analytical approach:

1. Data Collection  
   Macroeconomic indicators and financial market data were collected from public sources.

2. Data Cleaning and Preprocessing  
   Different datasets were standardized by date, frequency, and column structure.

3. Data Integration  
   All indicators were merged into a single monthly dataset.

4. Exploratory Data Analysis  
   Trends, relative performance, volatility, and sector differences were analyzed.

5. Visualization  
   Power BI and Python-based visuals were used to interpret the results.

6. Strategic Interpretation  
   Findings were translated into a practical investment decision framework based on different investor risk profiles.

## Key Findings

### 1. The macroeconomic environment is not stable

Inflation, policy rates, and deposit rates did not move at the same speed during the analyzed period. This shows that investment decisions cannot be based on a single macroeconomic assumption.

### 2. Nominal return is not the same as real protection

Some low-risk instruments may appear attractive in nominal terms, but they may fail to protect purchasing power when inflation remains high.

### 3. Major assets behave differently across the same period

USD/TRY, gram gold, and BIST100 followed different performance paths. This supports the idea that relying on a single asset class can increase risk.

### 4. Diversification is also necessary within equities

BIST100 is not a homogeneous structure. Sector indices such as food, chemicals, and transportation showed different return patterns, indicating that diversification should also be considered within equity exposure.

### 5. There is no single correct portfolio

The appropriate investment approach depends on the investor’s risk tolerance, time horizon, liquidity needs, and ability to tolerate volatility.

## Investor Profiles

The study evaluates the findings through three different investor profiles:

### Risk-Averse Investor

Focuses on capital protection, liquidity, and lower volatility. However, inflation risk and real return erosion should still be considered.

### Balanced Investor

Aims to combine protection and growth. Diversification across asset classes plays a central role in this profile.

### Risk-Tolerant Investor

Seeks higher growth potential and can tolerate more volatility. However, a protection layer should not be fully ignored.
